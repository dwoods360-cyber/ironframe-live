## Why this chapter matters

Controls only help if you can find them. `/audit` is where practitioners map on-screen controls to handbook language.

## Learning objectives

- Open `/audit` with an allowed role (or record the redirect).
- List three controls or panels you can see.
- Map one control to a plain-English purpose sentence.

## How to get there

1. Sign in with a role your trainer allows for `/audit`.
2. Open `/audit`.
3. Scan meta-audit / control panels.
4. Optional: open `/docs/technical/security-and-compliance` if present in your corpus.

## Lab — Control mapping (PCORE-001)

For **each** of three UI controls/panels, answer all five:

1. What risk does it reduce?  
2. Preventive / detective / corrective?  
3. Framework anchor (SOC 2 criterion or NIST CSF function)?  
4. What evidence would prove operation?  
5. Likely owner role?

Also note role used and whether `/audit` access was denied.  
Do not change production controls without trainer approval.  
See [framework-control-depth.md](../manuals/framework-control-depth.md).
