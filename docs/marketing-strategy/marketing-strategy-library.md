## Category design & positioning

### Play Bigger — Category Design (Ramadan, Peterson, Lochhead, Maney)

- **Core:** Define a new category before competing in an old one.
- **Ironframe application:** "Quantitative GRC command post" — not another heatmap GRC or bolt-on AI chat.
- **Tactics:** Name the category, teach the problem, publish POV content, anchor proof on ALE + Irongate + agent observability.

### Obviously Awesome — Positioning (April Dunford)

- **Core:** Competitive alternatives → unique attributes → value → target segment → market category.
- **Ironframe application:** Alternatives = spreadsheets, legacy GRC, generic GRC+AI. Unique = BigInt ALE, zero-trust ingest, 19-agent observable workforce.
- **Tactics:** One positioning statement per beachhead (BHC subsidiary isolation, NERC evidence, MSSP enclaves).

### Positioning — The Battle for Your Mind (Ries & Trout)

- **Core:** Own a word or idea in the prospect's mind; simplicity wins.
- **Ironframe application:** Own "quantified board risk" vs "compliance checkbox."
- **Tactics:** Repeat category phrase in every asset; avoid feature laundry lists.

### Crossing the Chasm (Geoffrey A. Moore)

- **Core:** Beachhead concentration; whole product for pragmatists.
- **Ironframe application:** One vertical until 3+ reference logos; whole product = GRC + ALE + exports + tenant isolation.
- **Tactics:** Case studies by sector; peer proof before adjacent expansion.

## Story-driven messaging (Donald Miller — StoryBrand)

See [storybrand-framework.md](./storybrand-framework.md) for SB7, BrandScript, one-liner, and website wireframe.

- **Building a StoryBrand:** Customer is hero; brand is guide; clarity beats cleverness.
- **Marketing Made Simple:** Five-part sales funnel (one-liner → lead generator → nurture → sales email → website).

## Persuasion & memorability

### Influence (Robert Cialdini)

- **Six principles:** Reciprocity, commitment/consistency, social proof, authority, liking, scarcity.
- **Ironframe application:** Lead with insight (reciprocity); cite design-partner proof (social proof); TAS/release evidence (authority).
- **Compliance:** No false scarcity or fabricated logos; label demo tenants as illustrative.

### Made to Stick (Chip & Dan Heath)

- **SUCCESs:** Simple, Unexpected, Concrete, Credible, Emotional, Stories.
- **Ironframe application:** Concrete = cents not floats; Credible = test gates and release SHA; Stories = agent roster solving named failure modes.

### Contagious (Jonah Berger)

- **STEPPS:** Social currency, Triggers, Emotion, Public, Practical value, Stories.
- **Ironframe application:** Practical value posts (ALE explainer, Irongate primer) outperform generic thought leadership.

## Inbound & content marketing

### They Ask, You Answer (Marcus Sheridan)

- **Core:** Answer every question buyers ask — honestly — on the website.
- **Ironframe application:** Docs hub, trust center, comparison pages with Shipped/Pilot/Roadmap labels.

### This Is Marketing (Seth Godin)

- **Core:** Marketing is change; smallest viable audience; tension + enrollment.
- **Ironframe application:** Design-partner enrollment for regulated mid-market; not mass-market self-serve until Phase 2.

### Traction — Bullseye Framework (Weinberg & Mares)

- **Core:** Test channels; double down on what moves SQL cost.
- **Ironframe application:** Prioritize trigger OSINT + executive roundtables + MSSP referrals over paid social spray.

## Product-led & habit (use sparingly for enterprise GRC)

### Hooked (Nir Eyal)

- **Core:** Trigger → Action → Variable reward → Investment.
- **Ironframe application:** Operator habit loops inside Command Center (integrity checks, export workflows) — not consumer gamification.

## Challenger & commercial insight (marketing alignment)

### The Challenger Sale (Dixon & Adamson)

- **Core:** Teach, tailor, take control — lead with insight that reframes buyer thinking.
- **Ironframe application:** "Beyond the heatmap" narrative; board dollar-risk vs compliance theater.

## Compliance in all marketing

- No guaranteed certification claims unless contracted.
- Simulation/shadow features labeled "staging" in public copy.
- Financial baselines (Medshield, Vaultbank, Gridcore) labeled **illustrative demo tenants**.
- Competitive claims: cite `docs/sales/competitive-analysis.md`; label Shipped / Pilot / Roadmap.
