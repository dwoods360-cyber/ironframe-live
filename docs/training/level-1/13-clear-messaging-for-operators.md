# Chapter 13 — Clear Messaging for Operators

> **Track:** LEVEL_1 · **Author agent:** board-trainer · **Release:** `v0.1.0-ga-epic17`
> **Target length:** ~1600 words · **Primary route:** `/docs`

## Learning objectives

After completing this chapter, you will recognize how Ironframe uses proven messaging frameworks (StoryBrand, Made to Stick) to keep **operator-facing** documentation clear — without turning training into sales pitches.

## Why operators need messaging literacy

You will see product copy, handbook headings, and in-app labels that follow a **problem → guide → plan → success** pattern. Understanding that structure helps you:

- Find the right doc faster in `/docs`
- Explain platform value to colleagues in plain language
- Avoid misreading demo tenant financials as real company data

**Quick tip:** Training covers clarity principles only. Sales campaigns, pricing, and pipeline tactics belong to IronBoard marketing agents — not this chapter.

## StoryBrand SB7 — operator translation

| StoryBrand element | What you see in the product |
|--------------------|----------------------------|
| Hero | You — the operator securing board-grade evidence |
| Problem | Audit pressure, unclear risk numbers, tenant isolation anxiety |
| Guide | Ironframe handbooks and Command Center workflows |
| Plan | Login → Integrity Hub → export audit trail (3-step pattern) |
| Success | Verifiable exports, labeled baselines, completed labs |

## Made to Stick — SUCCESs checklist for labs

When you write lab journal entries or help a peer:

1. **Simple** — one finding per bullet
2. **Unexpected** — note what surprised you (e.g., billing gate overlay)
3. **Concrete** — cite route paths and feature IDs, not vague "it worked"
4. **Credible** — anchor to `docs/TAS.md` or glossary entries
5. **Emotional** — optional: note stakeholder impact in plain terms
6. **Stories** — short narrative: "I opened /integrity and saw…"

## Hands-on lab — read messaging-aligned docs

### Lab 1: Locate strategy library (read-only)

1. Sign in to your assigned tenant.
2. Open `/docs` and search or browse for marketing-strategy content **if** your role includes internal strategy docs.
3. If strategy docs are not in your reader scope, open `user-manuals/quickstart` and note how the quickstart opens with **orientation** (guide) not feature dumps.
4. Record in your lab journal: one heading that states the operator problem before the procedure.

### Lab 2: Glossary discipline

1. Pick one acronym from a handbook (ALE, WORM, MSSP).
2. Write a **one-sentence** plain definition without marketing adjectives.
3. Compare to `user-manuals/glossary` if available.
4. **Quick tip:** If jargon appears in UI without a glossary link, file a support note — clarity is a product requirement.

### Lab 3: Demo tenant labels

1. Open `/integrity` and locate Medshield, Vaultbank, or Gridcore baseline cards.
2. Confirm UI or docs label these as **illustrative demo tenants**.
3. Practice explaining to a colleague: "These are synthetic baselines for training — not our company's real financials."
