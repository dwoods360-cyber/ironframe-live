# Chapter 13 — Clear Lab Notes for Ironframe Core

> **Track:** LEVEL_1 · **Reading level:** 11th grade · **Release:** `v0.1.0-ga-epic17`  
> **Primary route:** `/docs` · **Lab IDs:** MSG-001

## Why this chapter matters

Clear notes help you learn Ironframe Core faster. Student manuals use simple patterns so procedures guide the learner — not a sales pitch.

## Learning objectives

When you finish, you can:

- Spot problem → guide → plan → success in student handbook copy.
- Write lab notes that are short and concrete.
- Keep demo tenant money separate from real company talk.

## How to get there

1. Open `/docs/training/student` and open the [classroom quick-start](/docs/training/student/manuals/quickstart).
2. Find one heading that states a problem before a procedure.
3. Open the [student glossary](/docs/training/student/manuals/glossary) and rewrite one acronym in your own words.
4. Open `/integrity` and practice saying seed baselines are training fixtures.

## Lab — Clarity drills (MSG-001)

1. Copy one handbook heading that names the Core problem first.
2. Write a one-sentence plain definition of ALE or WORM with no marketing adjectives.
3. Explain to a peer (or journal): Medshield/Vaultbank/Gridcore are synthetic training baselines.
4. Rewrite a vague lab note like 'it worked' into a note with a route and a finding.

## Check your understanding

- [ ] I can find the problem statement in a student handbook page.
- [ ] I can write a concrete lab note with a route.
- [ ] I never present seed baselines as real customer financials.

## Common mistakes

- This chapter is clarity practice for Core labs. It is not a sales or pricing lab.
- Do not turn training notes into campaign copy or Ops Hub GTM work.
