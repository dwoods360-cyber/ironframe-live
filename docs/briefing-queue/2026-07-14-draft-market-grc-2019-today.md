> **Executive Summary:** The modern GRC landscape is defined by the limits of point-in-time checklists and the rise of continuous, telemetry-driven, quantitative control. This briefing analyzes how regimes such as DORA and disclosure enforcement actions raise the bar for operational resilience — and why organizations need defensible dollar risk and workspace isolation, not merely faster certification theater.

### I. Exposure Vector

Threat execution speed collapses the utility of annual checklist passes. Regulations such as DORA and related digital operational resilience expectations push toward continuous evidence, logging, and tested response. Parallel AI-agent deployment introduces privilege drift and poorly attested decision paths when controls live outside the systems that execute them.

Organizations that rely only on qualitative High/Medium/Low heatmaps cannot defend exposure in financial terms. Threat intake, control evidence, and auditor handoff fracture across consoles and spreadsheets — breaking the threat-to-risk chain.

Continuous GRC requires deterministic orchestration: validated ingress, workspace-scoped state, and automated assistants locked to controlled generation settings so remediation stays auditable.
