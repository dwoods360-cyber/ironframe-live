# Chapter 1 — Read the hazard pipeline

> **Track:** ANALYST · **Reading level:** 11th grade · **Release:** `v0.1.0-ga-epic17`  
> **Primary route:** `/integrity` · **Lab IDs:** ANALYST-001

## Why this chapter matters

A junior GRC analyst starts by **seeing risk in the product**, not in a slide deck. Integrity Hub shows the hazard pipeline and ALE posture for the active tenant.

## Learning objectives

When you finish, you can:

- Open `/integrity` and find the hazard pipeline.
- Write one hazard in plain words (what it is, why it matters).
- Note the ALE / exposure language shown for the tenant (dollars, not raw cents).

## How to get there

1. Sign in with classroom credentials.
2. Open `/integrity`.
3. Find the hazard pipeline (or active risk list) in the Command Deck.
4. Find the ALE or financial posture area.

## GRC knowledge (apply from manuals)

Before the click lab, write in your journal:

- Threat vs vulnerability for this hazard (best guess from the label).  
- Inherent vs residual (what you can see vs what you cannot yet see).  
- One preventive and one detective control that *could* apply.

Review: [risk-and-ale.md](../manuals/risk-and-ale.md).

## Lab — Pick one hazard (ANALYST-001)

1. Choose **one** hazard or active risk card on screen.
2. Journal: route · hazard name/label · one sentence impact · time.
3. Write the tenant name you are viewing.
4. Mark whether the page is live Core data or a PILOT demo surface.
5. Add a mini risk-register line: asset · threat · treatment idea (mitigate/transfer/avoid/accept) · owner (role).

## Check your understanding

- [ ] I can point to the hazard pipeline on `/integrity`.
- [ ] I described one hazard without marketing adjectives.
- [ ] I read dollars from the UI, not database dumps.

## Common mistakes

- Do not invent hazards that are not on screen.
- Seed tenant money is a training fixture — say so in your journal.
