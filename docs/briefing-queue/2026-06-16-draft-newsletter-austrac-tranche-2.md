> **Executive Summary:** Tranche 2 is a readiness problem before it is a reporting problem. Businesses that will provide newly regulated designated services from 1 July 2026 need an implemented AML/CTF program, a named compliance officer, trained personnel, customer due diligence procedures, and a path to AUSTRAC enrolment by 29 July 2026. Coverage turns on designated services with a geographical link to Australia—not on job title alone.

## I. Exposure Vector

A mid-size professional practice sees “Tranche 2” headlines and assumes either that everyone in the industry is automatically covered or that enrolment alone equals compliance. Both errors create governance failure.

The correct June questions are:

* Do we provide (or will we provide from 1 July 2026) a designated service with a geographical link to Australia?
* Is a board- or senior-management-approved AML/CTF program written and operable—not merely drafted?
* Who is the AML/CTF compliance officer, and can that appointment be notified through enrolment?
* Can staff apply CDD and escalate suspicious matters before the first regulated engagement after commencement?
* What enrolment deadline applies (generally 29 July 2026 for new designated services commencing 1 July 2026)? [1][2]

AUSTRAC’s public materials state that newly regulated sectors include real estate, conveyancing, legal services, accounting, and precious stones and metals, and that newly regulated businesses must enrol by 29 July 2026. Obligations include implementing AML/CTF programs, conducting customer due diligence, reporting suspicious matters, and keeping relevant records. [2]

**Caution:** Not every lawyer, accountant, real-estate professional, or jeweller is automatically a reporting entity. Use AUSTRAC’s scoping tools and designated-service definitions before asserting coverage. [3]

## II. Quantitative Context

| Readiness clock | Date / rule of thumb | Governance meaning |
| --------------- | -------------------- | ------------------ |
| Obligation commencement for new designated services | 1 July 2026 | Program, CDD, training, and reporting duties are live |
| Enrolment for new designated services | By 29 July 2026 | Enrolment is mandatory but not a substitute for the program |
| Enrolment after starting a designated service | Generally within 28 days of starting | Ongoing rule for entities commencing later [1] |
| Enrolment detail changes | Within 14 days | Keep AUSTRAC Online current [3] |

There is no single “industry average fine” that substitutes for entity-specific exposure. The practical quantitative frame for a governing body is: number of designated-service lines in scope; volume of higher-risk clients; time-to-enrol; and gaps between written program and operable controls.

## III. What Modern GRC Must Enforce

### Recommended operating sections for newly regulated entities

| Topic | Minimum readiness evidence |
| ----- | -------------------------- |
| Who is newly regulated | Documented scoping memo: services mapped to designated-service definitions; geographic link analysis; out-of-scope rationale |
| AML/CTF program | Senior-management approval; governing-body oversight; ML/TF risk assessment; policies and procedures proportionate to risk |
| Compliance officer | Written appointment; day-to-day compliance ownership; AUSTRAC communication role |
| Personnel training | Role-based training completed before or at commencement; attendance records |
| Customer due diligence | KYC/beneficial ownership/PEP and sanctions screening workflows; escalation paths |
| Suspicious-matter reporting | Clear internal escalation; ability to lodge required reports via AUSTRAC Online |
| Enrolment and transition | AUSTRAC Online enrolment plan to meet 29 July 2026 (or applicable 28-day rule); registration only if remittance/VASP rules apply [1][2] |
| Governance evidence retained | Risk assessment, approvals, training logs, CDD files, reports, independent evaluation schedule where required |

### Control translation

| Failure mode | Control requirement |
| ------------ | ------------------- |
| “We are a law firm, so we are in” / “we are out” without analysis | Service-by-service scoping with retained rationale |
| Enrolment without a live program | Treat enrolment and program implementation as parallel workstreams |
| Officer named only on a slide | Formal appointment and notification through enrolment process |
| CDD invented after the first suspicious client | Procedures, systems, and training operable at commencement |

Institutional programs consolidating this evidence frequently keep scoping decisions, approvals, and training records in a governed repository so the entity can show what it knew and approved before 1 July—not only what it filed after.

## IV. Verification Protocol

1. Confirm AUSTRAC enrolment guidance: enrol by 29 July 2026 if providing new designated services commencing 1 July 2026; generally enrol within 28 days of starting a designated service. [1]
2. Confirm AUSTRAC’s 1 July 2026 “new reporting regime” announcement describing newly regulated sectors and core obligations. [2]
3. Do not claim automatic coverage of entire professions; verify designated services. [3]
4. Keep this newsletter distinct from APRA CPS 230 (prudential operational risk)—different regulator, different perimeter.
5. Remittance and virtual asset service providers: confirm whether both enrolment and registration apply. [1][2]

## Key Takeaways

* June is for readiness: scoping, program, officer, training, CDD, and enrolment planning.
* 1 July starts obligations; 29 July is the enrolment hard date for new designated services commencing that day.
* Designated services—not job titles—determine whether Tranche 2 applies.
