## Why this chapter matters

Buyers and security reviewers want subprocessors and residency facts in one place. Trust Center is that packet.

## Learning objectives

When you finish, you can:

- Open `/trust`.
- Find residency and subprocessor information.
- Name the common subprocessors shown for classroom review.

## How to get there

1. Open `/trust`.
2. Review subprocessor and residency charts.
3. Find procurement or download links if present.
4. Note subprocessors such as Vercel, Supabase, and Resend when listed.

## Lab — Procurement facts card (TRUST-001)

1. Open `/trust`.
2. Write three bullets a buyer would care about.
3. List subprocessors you can verify on screen.
4. Note data residency claims in plain words.
5. Do not invent vendors that are not shown.

## Check your understanding

- [ ] I can open `/trust`.
- [ ] I can list at least two subprocessors from the page.
- [ ] I can explain why procurement cares about residency.

## Common mistakes

- Trust Center is facts for buyers. It is not a substitute for Integrity Hub risk work.
- Do not download or redistribute files outside class rules.
