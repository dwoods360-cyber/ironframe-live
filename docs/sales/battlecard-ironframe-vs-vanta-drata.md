# Battlecard — Ironframe vs Vanta / Drata

**Audience:** `board-sales-lead` + operator · design-partner conversations  
**Rule:** Credit competitors where they win; do not invent certifications or logos.

---

## One-line category split

| | **Vanta / Drata** | **Ironframe** |
|--|-------------------|---------------|
| **Buying job** | Audit **speed-to-cert**; integration catalog | **Financial risk defensibility** + **tenant sovereignty** |
| **Hero metric** | Controls mapped; continuously monitored | BigInt **ALE in cents**; isolated enclaves; governed exports |
| **Best fit** | SMB/mid-market SOC 2 / ISO runway | Multi-entity, MSSP, utilities, boards who ask “in dollars?” |

## When to walk or complement

- Buyer only wants **SOC 2 in 90 days** with hundreds of SaaS connectors → walk or position Ironframe **beside** their automation floor, not instead of it.  
- Buyer needs **board-pack dollar risk**, multi-tenant walls, zero-trust ingest → Ironframe wedge.

## Objection map

| They say | You say |
|----------|---------|
| “We’re already on Vanta/Drata.” | “Keep them for checklist continuous control. We quantify loss exposure and isolate entities — different job.” |
| “We need integrations.” | “Connector count isn’t our wedge. Irongate sanitizes ingress; we don’t become your integration marketplace.” |
| “Show me automation.” | “We show governed math, threat pipeline, and tenant-scoped exports — proof for auditors and boards.” |
| “Price?” | Design partner: Path B Command **$4,999**. Planned GA Command **~$35k/yr** — not per-seat SaaS. |

## Proof to bring

- Path B activation (tenant-scoped Stripe) → ACTIVE  
- Analyst export CSV from partner tenant  
- Order form success criteria (2–3) checked by day 90  

## Banned claims

- “Fastest SOC 2”  
- “We’re certified SOC 2” (unless true and evidenced)  
- Fake design-partner logos / fabricated scarcity  
- Demo tenant names as customer references
