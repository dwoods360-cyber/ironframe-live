> **Executive Summary:** Treat 2 August 2026 as an operational GRC threshold for transparency and related AI Act duties—not as a mythic date on which every high-risk obligation suddenly crystallises. Commission materials state that transparency rules come into effect in August 2026, while GPAI rules became effective earlier (August 2025) and prohibited practices earlier still (February 2025). High-risk applicability has been subject to Omnibus revision. Boards need evidence of what applies now, what was deferred, and how the organisation proves its scope and control choices.

## I. Exposure Vector

An enterprise uses generative assistants in customer service, scoring tools in credit or HR workflows, and multiple vendor models embedded in SaaS. Legal has an AI policy. Procurement has begun sending questionnaires. Engineering has no single inventory.

On 2 August 2026, the exposure is mismatched certainty:

* Teams assume “the AI Act starts today” for everything—or assume Omnibus delay means nothing must be evidenced.
* Article 50 transparency and labeling duties become a practical compliance surface for chatbots, synthetic content, and deepfake-related disclosures. [1]
* GPAI provider obligations and prohibited-practice bans may already apply from prior 2025 milestones. [1]
* High-risk Annex timelines may follow revised dates under the Omnibus process; until formal texts are confirmed, organisations must avoid asserting that all high-risk requirements become applicable on 2 August 2026. [2]

The governance problem is proving: (a) which systems are in scope; (b) whether the organisation is provider, deployer, importer, or distributor; (c) which transparency measures operate in production; (d) what human oversight and documentation exist; and (e) what alternative compliance measures are used where codes of practice are not signed. [1][3]

## II. Quantitative Context

| Milestone | Timing (verify against current Commission timeline) | Governance meaning |
| --------- | --------------------------------------------------- | ------------------ |
| Prohibited AI practices | From February 2025 | Ban compliance and screening of use cases [1] |
| GPAI model rules | From August 2025 | Provider transparency/copyright and systemic-risk duties where applicable [1] |
| Transparency obligations (incl. Article 50 themes) | From August 2026 | User-facing disclosure, machine-detectable marking / labeling expectations [1] |
| High-risk system obligations | Subject to Omnibus-revised schedule—confirm current law vs political agreement | Do not treat 2 Aug 2026 as universal high-risk go-live [2] |

Penalty maxima in the AI Act are turnover-linked and tiered by violation type; they are not a substitute for entity-specific exposure analysis. Boards should quantify: number of AI use cases, number without inventory owners, number of customer-facing systems lacking transparency UX, and number of vendor models without documented role allocation.

## III. What Modern GRC Must Enforce

### Recommended briefing structure (operating agenda)

| Section | Evidence to produce |
| ------- | ------------------- |
| Requirements already applicable since 2025 | Prohibited-practice screening; GPAI provider documentation where relevant |
| Provisions applying 2 August 2026 | Transparency/labeling controls for in-scope interactions and content |
| Article 50 transparency and labeling | Chatbot notices; synthetic content marking; deepfake/public-interest labeling where required |
| Provider vs deployer duties | Role matrix per system; contractual flow-downs |
| GPAI obligations | Training-data/transparency artifacts; systemic-risk assessments if triggered |
| Enforcement expectations | Market surveillance / AI Office interaction model; complaint and incident paths |
| Revised high-risk dates | Written memo citing current legal baseline and Omnibus status |
| Transition / grandfathering | Documented reliance on transitional provisions where available |
| Human oversight and documentation | Oversight design, escalation, logs |
| Evidence for scope decisions | Inventory, risk classification rationale, exclusions with approver |

### Control-system requirements

| Failure mode | Requirement |
| ------------ | ----------- |
| “Omnibus delayed everything” | Separate transparency/GPAI/prohibitions from high-risk deferrals |
| Inventory as a slide | Living register of systems, models, vendors, purposes, and roles |
| Transparency only in policy | Production UX and content-marking controls with test evidence |
| Vendor questionnaires unanswered | Minimum evidence pack for procurement renewals regardless of legislative deferral [2] |
| Code of Practice non-signatory without rationale | Documented alternative measures explaining compliance approach [3] |

### Soft institutional bridge

Operators addressing this vector often need governed AI inventories, attestation gates before external claims, and retained decision records for scope and transparency choices—so human accountability remains visible when automated systems generate customer-facing content.

## IV. Verification Protocol

1. Confirm Commission statement that transparency rules of the AI Act come into effect in August 2026, prohibited practices from February 2025, and GPAI rules from August 2025. [1]
2. Before asserting high-risk deadlines, verify whether Omnibus amendments have been formally adopted and published; distinguish political agreement from binding law. [2]
3. Do not state that all high-risk-system requirements become applicable on 2 August 2026.
4. Confirm provider/deployer definitions against Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 text on EUR-Lex for any system-specific advice.
5. Keep DORA, CSRD, CPS 230, AUSTRAC, and SEC Item 1.05 out of this briefing’s lead claims.

## Key Takeaways

* 2 August 2026 is real for transparency—and must not be mythologised as universal high-risk go-live.
* Prior 2025 milestones already created binding duties for bans and GPAI.
* Proof is inventory, roles, operating transparency controls, oversight, and documented scope decisions.
