## 🗺️ Section 1: Dashboard Layout & Monitor Ratios
When you load your workspace, the software organizes your entire screen into **one single control command center window** split vertically into three highly independent panels. 

As you drag or resize your web browser window, these columns scale fluids together while locking in their exact proportional width percentages to match your monitor size perfectly:

1. **The Left Column Panel [Takes up exactly 22% of your screen width]:**
   * **What it contains:** The Master Control Matrix, simulation bot toggles, and scenario drills parameters.
   * **How it scrolls:** This panel features independent vertical scrolling. Hovering your mouse over it allows you to scroll down through options without shifting any elements on the rest of the page.
2. **The Center Column Canvas [Takes up exactly 48% of your screen width]:**
   * **What it contains:** Core analytical data grids, system maturity track readings (showing `4.5 / 10`), and operational synchronization logs.
   * **How it scrolls:** This panel tracks independently down the page, giving you a spacious, wide room to read primary records.
3. **The Right Column Panel [Takes up exactly 30% of your screen width]:**
   * **What it contains:** Live operational alert feeds, global log search utilities, and your interactive sustainability instruments.
   * **How it scrolls:** This panel moves independently up and down so you can monitor live telemetry without losing your spot on the left controls.

## 🧪 Section 2: Core Step-by-Step Laboratory Workflows

Follow these step-by-step tracking actions on your computer screen to audit the live behaviors of the software.

### 🔐 Lab Module 1: Session Cookies & User Badges
* **Function Name:** Authentication Token Tracker (`AUTH-001`).
* **Where it is on screen:** Pinned permanently to the global slate header bar running across the top frame of your screen window.
* **What it is used for:** Making sure that when you access your control room, the system issues you a secure digital badge so it remembers your specific user account.
* **How to use and test it:**
  1. Open your provisioned workspace URL in your browser.
  2. Input your assigned student credentials to log into the main operations deck.
  3. Once your dashboard mounts on screen, right-click anywhere on the dark background space and select **Inspect** (or press F12).
  4. Navigate to the **Application** or **Storage** tab menu and select the **Cookies** row on the left sidebar list.
  5. **What to look for:** Look closely at your browser's inspect row. Verify that an encrypted session token entry has initialized cleanly, proving your user session is authenticated.

### 🔄 Lab Module 2: The Multi-Tenant Network Switcher
* **Function Name:** Command Center Dropdown Menu (`TENANT-001`).
* **Where it is on screen:** Positioned at the top-left corner of your dashboard workspace layout.
* **What it is used for:** Instantly swapping your view context between three completely separate corporate entities: **Medshield** (healthcare networks), **Vaultbank NV** (financial ledger tracks), and **Gridcore Infrastructure** (utility power grids).
* **How to use and test it:**
  1. Click the active dropdown element showing the current company name.
  2. Choose **Vaultbank NV** from the animated option row and observe the statistics grids.
  3. Click the dropdown text box a second time and select **Medshield**.
  4. **The Turbo-Click Stress Test:** Click that company box again and rapidly toggle back and forth between **Vaultbank** and **Gridcore** five times in a row.
  5. **What to look for:** The app should never freeze, crash, or show an empty white layout unmount error. When you select a company, the central panel grids must briefly flash a temporary gray loading skeleton box. Then, all data must transform instantly to show *only* that company's numbers. Row-Level Security ensures that banking records never bleed or leak into public infrastructure views!

### 🔋 Lab Module 3: Sustainability Telemetry
* **Function Name:** Sustainability Pulse Component (`CARBON-001`).
* **Where it is on screen:** Fixed inside the upper section of your Right Column Panel (taking up 30% of the screen space). Look for the green leaf icon and the panel header labeled **`SUSTAINABILITY PULSE`**.
* **What it is used for:** Monitoring real-time environmental footprint loads, power grid strain data, and hardware energy intensity metrics managed by the background `Ironbloom` agent.
* **Is it Information-Only? No! It is an active tracking instrument:** It dynamically changes its data in sync with your actions. It catches your tenant switcher signals to recalculate values, responds to active simulation parameters, and houses interactive contextual support drawers.
* **How to use and test it:**
  1. Look directly inside the panel body box to view the active intensity chart stream displaying **`382 gCO₂eq/kWh`**.
  2. Note the orange status badge on the top right labeled **`FALLBACK ACTIVE`**.
  3. **What to look for:** This badge proves that your application safety net is running properly behind the scenes. If you run your server locally without live internet connection API keys, the software catches the missing link and immediately switches to an integrated **Forensic Baseline** backup simulation graph instead of breaking.

### 📊 Lab Module 4: Financial Data & Core Ledgers
* **Function Name:** Analyst Portal CSV Export Ledger (`EXPORT-001`).
* **Where it is on screen:** Located inside your center dashboard area within the lower administrative option grids.
* **What it is used for:** Gathering precise, mathematically pristine tracking reports for platform auditors without decimal rounding errors.
* **How to use and test it:**
  1. Navigate to your Analyst interface tabs and find the bold panel element displaying three side-by-side metric boxes: `Compliance Velocity`, `Value Mitigated (YTD)`, and `Projected Insurance Savings`.
  2. Verify that these cards sit cleanly in a **horizontal left-to-right row** with uniform margins and spacing.
  3. Find and click the action trigger button labeled **`Export Tabular Ledger Data (CSV)`**.
  4. Open the downloaded file on your local desktop using a spreadsheet tool or standard text editor.
  5. **What to look for:** Look closely at the number columns. All monetary statistics must display as exact, whole whole integers representing cents (e.g., a balance of ten dollars will show up as `1000`). This demonstrates how enterprise-grade banking applications maintain 100% mathematical accuracy by avoiding decimal floats!

## 💡 Section 3: Interactive Help & Feature Glossary
If you ever get stuck or confused while working through your laboratory modules independently online, look for the inline help badges embedded natively throughout the application interface:

* **The Help Triggers:** Next to major module titles, look for a button labeled **`❓ HELP`**.
* **What happens when clicked:** Clicking this button opens an overlay window sliding out from the right margin of your display screen. It will tell you the exact **Function Name**, **On-Screen Location**, **What it is used for**, and **How to use it** instantly without closing your active work.
* **Deep Links:** Inside that help window, click the text button labeled **`OPEN DEEP-DIVE WEB DOCUMENT`** to open the complete feature glossary chapter in the documentation corpus (`docs/qa/complete-feature-glossary.md`).
