# Chapter 13 — Clear Messaging for Operators

> **Track:** LEVEL_1 · **Reading level:** 11th grade · **Release:** `v0.1.0-ga-epic17`  
> **Primary route:** `/docs` · **Lab IDs:** MSG-001

## Why this chapter matters

Clear docs help you learn faster. Ironframe uses simple story patterns so manuals guide the operator — not a sales pitch.

## Learning objectives

When you finish, you can:

- Spot problem → guide → plan → success in handbook copy.
- Write lab notes that are short and concrete.
- Keep demo tenant money separate from real company talk.

## How to get there

1. Open `/docs` and open the quick-start or operator packet.
2. Find one heading that states a problem before a procedure.
3. Open the glossary and rewrite one acronym in your own words.
4. Open `/integrity` and practice saying seed baselines are training fixtures.

## Lab — Clarity drills (MSG-001)

1. Copy one handbook heading that names the operator problem first.
2. Write a one-sentence plain definition of ALE or WORM with no marketing adjectives.
3. Explain to a peer (or journal): Medshield/Vaultbank/Gridcore are synthetic training baselines.
4. Rewrite a vague lab note like 'it worked' into a note with a route and a finding.

## Check your understanding

- [ ] I can find the problem statement in a handbook page.
- [ ] I can write a concrete lab note with a route.
- [ ] I never present seed baselines as real customer financials.

## Common mistakes

- This chapter is clarity practice. It is not a sales or pricing lab.
- Do not turn training notes into campaign copy.

## Glossary
