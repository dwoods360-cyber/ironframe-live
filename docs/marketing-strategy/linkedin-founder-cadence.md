# Founder-led LinkedIn cadence

**Purpose:** Build trust with GRC operators through useful, problem-led founder posts. This is a manual-publishing plan; it does not authorize automated posting or account activity.

## Weekly rhythm

| Day | Founder post | Audience outcome | Primary link |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monday | Name one persistent GRC problem and explain why the usual fix has not removed it. | Establish a clear control-first point of view. | `https://ironframegrc.com/marketing` or the founder's production-host equivalent |
| Wednesday | Show one short, bounded Ironframe workflow demonstration. | Make the workflow concrete without presenting demo data as customer proof. | `/product-demo` |
| Friday | Teach one practical control lesson derived from Governance Frame research. | Give the audience a repeatable control-design principle. | `/trust-center` or `/register/contact` |

## Publishing rules

1. Publish from the founder's personal profile first. The Ironframe company page amplifies the published post with a concise summary and the original-post link.
2. Write for the operator who owns evidence, risk, audit, or board reporting: problem → workflow → proof path. The reader is the hero; Ironframe is the guide.
3. Use one idea and one primary link per post. Prefer short paragraphs and three to five relevant hashtags.
4. Keep product demonstrations bounded to what the product can show. Demo tenants are demonstrations, not customers; never imply customer logos, pilot results, audit outcomes, or certifications.
5. Describe certification posture accurately. Do not claim SOC 2 or ISO certification; use “SOC 2-aligned” only where it is relevant and supportable.
6. Do not frame the offer as “Request Demo,” a free pilot, freemium, or per-seat/month pricing. The consistent CTA is a **10–15 minute workflow review** focused on evidence or board-reporting pain.
7. Mention the paid Path B design-partner on-ramp only when commercial context is useful and accurate: **$4,999**, **90-day** scoped window, 2–3 success criteria, and 3–5 co-builders. Do not use it as a blanket CTA.
8. Product links may be relative (`/product-demo`, `/register/contact`, `/trust-center`) while drafting. The founder should replace them with the correct production host before publishing when LinkedIn requires an absolute URL.

## Newsletter

Publish one recurring founder newsletter: **Control-First GRC**. Its job is to turn a recurring research theme into a practical operator lesson—not to restate a product announcement. A useful edition structure is:

1. The persistent operating problem
2. Why common process or tooling fixes fail
3. One control-first workflow or design test
4. A small “what to inspect this week” checklist
5. An applied-governance note pointing to `/product-demo` or `/register/contact`

## Content guardrails from Governance Frame

Recurring themes may include fragmented evidence, qualitative heatmaps that fail board scrutiny, untrusted ingest, cosmetic multi-entity isolation, and unbounded AI drafting. Refer to Governance Frame research at a high level unless a human reviewer supplies the original, verified citation and publishable link. Do not turn quarantined research drafts into public citations or fabricate research URLs.
